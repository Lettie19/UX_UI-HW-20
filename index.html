Improving The ICAF's user engagement

Revitalize a non-profit's flagship program and donations pages to be more intuitive and responsive.

Overview

The ICAF is a non-profit organization located in Washington, DC.
Their focus is to grow creativity and empathy in children. They have a firmbelief that those two values have a unique relationship that is important for children to develop, and they aim to do that through art.

The objecive for the project was to redesign four distinct pages from teh website with a focus on optimizing the information structure and donations page to ensure that it was consistent, intuitive and usable.

Duration

3 weeks

Client

  • UX/UI Designer
  • Prototyping & testing
  • Visual design - completed 80% of the UI for all pages (colors, picutres, icons, layout)
  • Research plan and Stakeholder interview
  • Contributed to all steps of the UX research

Team

3 UX/UI designers

Tools

The problem

We have observed that the current design layout and information structure of the website aren’t meeting these goals which
is causing busy and impatient users to waste time attempting to locate information and potentially miss out on possible
events/opportunities to donate and participate.

How might we improve the design of the international Child Art Foundation’s website so that users are able to
easily locate information about the organization, its initiatives and opportunities to support?

The Solution

We focused our redesign on three key features based on our user research results and brainstorming sessions:

  • UX/UI Designer
  • We increased usability by improving the content hierarchy to make it easier for users to accomplish the task of locating information specific to their
    needs on the website.
  • Optimized the website navigation bar
  • Finally, we modernized the layout of the website by adding in more color, using modern typography and increasing negative space between
    text and cards.

01. Research

Stakeholder's Goals

Our research started with me interviewing The ICAF’s founder to find out what the ICAF is all about. This was incredibly helpful, as it enabled us to
gain more insight into the organization and put emphasis on the things most important to the organization. I also remained in constant communication
with the organizaton’s CFO to inform them of our progress and confirm some design decision . Key insights from the interview:

  • Website has to be both playful and academic
  • How can the site better drive donations?
  • Ensure redesign includes flagship event - 6th Arts Olympiad

Heuristics Analysis

After talking to our client, we conducted a heuristic evaluation of the ICAF’S websited for usability, accessibility, and universal
design principle. We observed:

  • Color contrast made it challenging for users to read text
  • The page didn't have much content hierarchy
  • The information architecture wasn't clear and consistent

Qualitative Interviews

We conducted user interviews and asked four users to complete three key tasks to assess the functionality of the current site. The research helped us
understand the needs of our users and also validated the findings from our heuristic analysis.

User Insights

After gathering and analyzing the data from our user interviews, we were able to summarize them into these key
insights help us understand our user pain points.

  • The navigation bar isn't clear and is misleading
  • The custom 404 error message is unclear
  • The pages are heavy on text and lack clarity and hierarchy
  • The organizatin's mission is unclear from looking at the landing page

From our testing, we focused on over 25 data points we wanted to include in our redesign. Taking into consideration our stakeholder’s goals and the
results of our testing. we decided on three key features for the redesign to have a modern site layout, consistent navigation throughout the site,
and better organization of the content across the site.

We were able to prioritize these features mainly by understanding and empathizing with our user, Joe Stephens. Joe is a public art teacher
and he thrives off of the passion he sees when his students can express themselves through their work. This has been especially difficult with virtual
learning and he has seen the impact it has had on his students. He started looking for more ways to inspire his kids and thought the ICAF would be
the best to accomplish that.

02. Ideation

In the redesign process we were inspired not only by the organization’s mission which is centered around children and the arts and also our
stakeholder’s goals to have the site be a little bit more colorful and engaging. We selected a color palette that communicated those objectives and
choose a typography that communicated more of aprofessional tone.

Style Guide

Sitemap

With a better understanding of our user journey, we conducted a card sorting exercise and redesigned the sitemap to be more intuitive and
organized. We wanted to o ensure that the navigation for the site was easier and more straightforward for those parents, students and
teachers that would be interested in participating in one of the ICAF’s programs. We also wanted any potential donors or sponsors to quickly
access resources and pertinent info about the organization.

03. Prototyping & Testing

Early Concepts

Using the insights we gathered from our research, the team started createing user flows.
We started sketching the information architecture and lo-fi concept to be used for our redesign. After reviewing our designs against our research and once
we were confident about our sketchees, we began digitalizing them and I designed our mid and hi-fi prototypes.

We conducted several levels of testing with 8 users to determine if our design was solving the user’s pain points discovered during our research
phase. We made a couple of iterations to our designs based on the feedback from our usability testing.

04. The Solution

A new redesigned , usable, easy to navigate and vibrant site to increase user engagement and donations. We comlpeted our redesign of the four pages we are set out to improve and were confident to present the final product to our stakeholder
after the last round of testing had a 100% completion rate for all tasks.

Responsive Web Design

We focused on buidling the website as that was our main goal within the time frame we were given. However, with some days left before
submission, we were able to design a mobile version of the website. We designed two unique mobile layouts for the landing page and tested them for
UI preference and navigation with 24 users. I designed layout A and my teammate designed the more interactive layout B.

Additional Screens

As a final step, we presented the final redesign to our stakeholder and his webmaster who both approved of the redesigned and confirmed that we
targeted and resolved the key pain points as per our initial interview and also based on data from research. Elements of the redesign
will be implemented on their final redesign.

05. Further Development

Key Learnings

  • Usability > Beautitful design - As our UI preference reaffirmed, as UX/UI designers, we have to make sure that our we do not place a
    beautiful design over user experience. The designer’s objective is first to ensure that the app is easy to use, intuite and usable. It is important
    to already consider and reassess the reason behind design decisions.
  • Qualitative data for different types of users’ insights - Adding a survey in our research migth have provided us with more insight from
    different user groups that the website target - more specifically students and children. It would also have been helpful in creating a different
    user persona who is a child. Unfortunately, without much time and access to children, we were not able to gather that data.
  • Time Management is Essential - As we juggled our office day jobs, night classes and project deadlines, it became even more clear to us after
    the third day on the project that regular standups and updating of our Trello board was necessary to keep track of our deliverables and ensure
    that we completed all our research and data analysis and synthesis in time for us to begin sketching and digitalizing our designs.

Next Steps

  • Logo Redesign - We initially thought about adding some color to the logo to make it more fitting to the brand of the organziation but our
    client was not sure about the idea. I would consider proposing different iterated versions of the logo to our client to provide him with a visual
    that may give him a better understanding of the vision and see if he would want to proceed in that direction.
  • Break down the donation page into different pages with progress indicator - The current donation page could be broken down into
    different pages to reduce the amount of information the users have to see from first landing on the page. An A/B testing or user preference
    test would determine whether the change allows for better user experience.
